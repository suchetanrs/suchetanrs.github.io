Robot Software Engineer | Clutterbot
Jul '24 – Present, Bangalore, India

I own the navigation software stack for Clutterbot’s consumer robots, delivering a scalable framework with runtime-swappable planners and controllers, rich telemetry, and robust failure handling. I led the design of a behavior tree-based navigation handler with a recovery action server for context-aware error recovery and developed an extension of the Graceful Controller law for dynamic obstacle avoidance, increasing docking success to 95% in dynamic scenes.

Previously, as a Robot Software Intern, I built a probabilistic perception pipeline that fuses semantically segmented depth imagery with LiDAR for dynamic global re-planning in cluttered environments, and a multi-objective approach-pose estimator that jointly optimizes object scale, traversability, manipulation effort, and clutter for robust pre-grasp pose selection. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Research Collaborator | NaviR2eS, ISAE-SUPAERO
Jan '24 – Present, Toulouse, France (Remote)

I collaborate with the NaviR2eS research team on information- and traversability-aware exploration for unmanned ground vehicles. I led the development of FIT-SLAM 2, an active exploration framework with a novel adaptive roadmap, TSP-based goal scheduling, and real-time Fisher information computation, achieving a 34% higher exploration rate than state-of-the-art baselines.

I also designed a dynamic speed profiling heuristic that improves travel-time estimation and safety in unstructured environments, resulting in a 10% reduction in traversal time and a 44% improvement in safety. This work builds on my bachelor’s thesis, FIT-SLAM, which combines Fisher information-based utility with traversability mapping for exploration in 3D environments.

Undergraduate Researcher | BITS Pilani & ISAE-SUPAERO
Jul '23 – Dec '23, Bachelor’s Thesis

For my thesis, titled “Fisher information and traversability-estimation-based active SLAM for exploration in 3D environments”, I conducted an extensive survey of active SLAM and exploration methods, identifying gaps in information-, localization-, and traversability-aware exploration. I then architected and implemented FIT-SLAM, an exploration module that fuses Fisher information with a traversability layer to jointly reason about localization quality and terrain risk.

This work led to multiple first-author contributions, including FIT-SLAM (ICARA 2024), FIT-SLAM 2 (Robotics and Autonomous Systems, 2025), and traversability-aware planning with dynamic speed profiling for planetary exploration (iSpaRo 2025).

Manipulation Subsystem Lead | Project Kratos, BITS Pilani
May '21 – May '23

I led the manipulation subsystem for Project Kratos, BITS Pilani’s Mars rover team, focusing on reliable end-effector control and perception for field robotics competitions.
